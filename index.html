Predicting Emotional Intensity in Political Debates via Non-verbal Signals

Authors: Jeewoo Yoon, Jinyoung Han, Erik Bucy, and Jungseock Joo

Abstract: Non-verbal communications of political leaders have received great attention. In particular, scholars have studied relationships between the emotional intensity of politicians and voters' evaluation. However, most prior works measured the emotional intensity via vocal pitch, which may not capture the comprehensive information of one’s emotional intensity. To address this issue, We develop a multimodal deep-learning model that utilizes voice, face, and gesture to predict the perceived emotional intensity of a candidate from a video clip. We collect a dataset of political debate videos from the 2020 Democratic presidential primaries, and train the proposed model with randomly sampled clips from the debate videos. By applying the proposed model to 23 candidates in 11 debate videos, we show that the standard deviation of the perceived emotional intensity is positively correlated with the changes in candidates’ favorability in public polls.
